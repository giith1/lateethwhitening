At LA Teeth Whitening, Tooth whitening or tooth lightening is the process of lightening the colour of human teeth. Whitening is commonly desirable when teeth come to be yellowed with time for a variety of factors, and can be achieved by transforming the innate or extrinsic colour of the tooth enamel. The chemical degradation of the chromogens within or on the tooth is described as bleaching. Hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) is the active component most generally made use of in lightening products and is delivered as either hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide.

Hydrogen peroxide is similar to carbamide peroxide as it is released when the stable facility is in contact with water. When it diffuses right into the tooth, hydrogen peroxide serves as an oxidising agent that breaks down to produce unpredictable cost-free radicals. In the rooms in between the not natural salts in tooth enamel, these unstable cost-free radicals affix to organic pigment molecules resulting in little, much less heavily pigmented components. Showing much less light, these smaller sized molecules create a "lightening result".

Peroxyacids are an alternative to hydrogen peroxide and additionally contribute to the failure of pigment molecules. There are various products available on the market to remove stains. For bleaching therapy to be successful, dental specialists (oral hygienist or dentist) ought to appropriately identify the kind, intensity and area of the tooth discolouration. Time exposure and the focus of the lightening substance figures out the tooth bleaching endpoint.

Achieving Whiter Teeth: With LA Teeth Whitening

A radiant, white smile is often associated with health and beauty. Many people turn to cosmetic treatments to achieve that ideal smile, and the UK offers a range of options.

From over-the-counter products to professional private care, there are several ways to whiten your teeth.

The best approach can enhance your enamel's appearance, reduce discoloration, and help maintain healthy gums while preventing tooth sensitivity.

In this article, we explore some popular whitening methods and key things to consider before making your decision.

Laser Whitening

A highly popular choice for achieving brighter teeth is opting for professional treatment, which requires a visit to a dentist. Cosmetic dentistry often uses peroxide-based products, such as hydrogen peroxide, to address stains caused by red wine, coffee, or tobacco. Two common techniques include laser whitening and the use of bleaching gels.

Laser whitening is an advanced treatment that can lighten teeth in just one session. During this procedure, a bleaching gel is applied to the enamel, and a laser helps activate the gel, accelerating the whitening process. This method is especially effective for tough stains and discoloration. However, it’s important to discuss potential side effects, such as tooth sensitivity, with your dentist.

Tooth sensitivity is a common reaction to whitening treatments since many of them involve exposing the enamel to bleaching agents like peroxide. Dentists may suggest fluoride treatments or desensitizing gels to alleviate discomfort. It's also crucial to use these treatments properly to avoid damaging your gums, as incorrect application could lead to further problems.

Philips Zoom Whitening

The Philips Zoom system is a cutting-edge whitening treatment that uses LED light in conjunction with a specially designed bleaching gel. It is widely regarded as one of the top choices for safe and effective teeth whitening.

The entire procedure is typically finished in just one visit, providing you with noticeably whiter teeth right away.

At-Home Whitening Kits

If going to the dentist isn't an option, at-home whitening kits have become a popular alternative. These typically come with trays filled with bleaching gels or strips containing hydrogen peroxide to help remove stains. While these products can deliver visible results, they may not be ideal for everyone, particularly those with sensitive teeth.

It's always a good idea to consult with a dental hygienist or a dentist registered with the General Dental Council to ensure they are safe for use.

 

Choosing the Right Care in the UK

Cities like Bristol in the UK offer a variety of cosmetic dentistry options, from traditional teeth whitening to implants and veneers. It’s important to select a qualified dentist who understands your specific dental needs and any associated risks.

Make sure your dentist is registered with the General Dental Council, as this ensures a focus on both safety and cosmetic outcomes. While achieving a brighter smile can improve confidence and oral health, it’s crucial to explore your options and consult with your dentist to ensure the best possible results tailored to your smile.

LA Teeth Whitening FAQs
1. Is Teeth Whitening Expensive? How Much Does It Cost?

Teeth whitening prices vary based on promotions and treatment options. For the most up-to-date pricing, visit our Teeth Whitening Prices page, where we update offers monthly. The prices you see there are exactly what we charge—no hidden fees!

2. Does Laser Teeth Whitening Hurt?

Not at all! Most clients experience little to no sensitivity during or after the treatment. We apply a specially formulated gel before and after the whitening process, reducing sensitivity by over 60%.

3. Whats the Difference Between Zoom and Laser Teeth Whitening?

Both treatments work in the same way. Zoom and Laser Teeth Whitening use a special light to activate the whitening gel, speeding up the process. The entire procedure takes just one hour for noticeable results.

4. Are There Any Side Effects?

The only known side effect is mild tooth sensitivity, which typically lasts just a few hours. Otherwise, the treatment is completely safe.

5. Am I a Suitable Candidate for Teeth Whitening?

Most adults over 18 are eligible for laser teeth whitening. However, you should not undergo the treatment if you are:

  • Pregnant

  • Under 18 years old

  • Diagnosed with epilepsy

We also recommend consulting your dentist before booking an appointment. On the day of your treatment, we conduct a brief pre-consultation to confirm suitability.

6. How Long Does the Treatment Take?

The entire process, including the pre-consultation and whitening treatment, takes about 90 minutes. This allows for optimal whitening results while ensuring your comfort.
